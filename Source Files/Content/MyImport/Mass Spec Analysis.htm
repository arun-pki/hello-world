Mass spec analysis

A fragmentation line consists of one or more straight lines that overlap bonds in a structure. These lines are visual aids only, and do not break bonds within the structure.
To draw a fragmentation line, click Mass Spec Analysis tool on the Fragmentation toolbar and drag. To stop drawing, release the mouse button.

 

To reposition one of the vertices of a line segment, press SHIFT and move the cursor over the line vertex and drag the vertex to the desired location.
By default, fragmentation lines appear red. To change the color, select the object and choose a color from the Color menu.
Fragmentation lines are grouped with the structure that they intersect. When you double-click an atom or bond with the Lasso selection tool, the fragmentation lines are also selected. This lets you rotate or translate the fragmentation lines with their associated structure. However, they can also be selected and moved independently.
By default, hovering over an atom or bond highlights the smallest fragment that contains the atom or bond, and the formula, exact mass, and mass of isotopes of significant abundance for each of two fragments are displayed. However, you can press the TAB key to iteratively select successively larger fragments.
